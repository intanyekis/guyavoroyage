How to spell cauldron

Aug 6, 2024 Which is correct caldron or cauldron? A cauldron (or caldron) is a large pot (kettle) for cooking or boiling over an open fire, with a lid and frequently with an arc-shaped hanger. In other words, a large kettle. Is cauldron a real word? The cauldron, while conjuring up images of — well, conjuring — actually came from the Latin root calidus, meaning “hot.” We can see this root in other Latin-based languages, including the Spanish caliente and Italian caldo.
