Aubrey Anderson-Emmons is an American actress and voice actress who is best known for having been cast as Lily Tucker-Pritchett in the mockumentary family romantic comedy series, Modern Family. She got cast on the show from the 3rd season onwards as the role was previously played by child actors Jaden Hiller and Ella Hiller. On Modern Family, Aubrey Anderson-Emmons co-stars alongside the likes of Ed O’Neill, Sofía Vergara, Julie Bowen, Ty Burrell, Jesse Tyler Ferguson, Sarah Hyland, Ariel Winter, and Nolan Gould and also became the youngest star to walk the red carpet at the Primetime Emmy Awards in 2012 and 2013. Apart from this, she has also appeared in a number of shows such as Bill Nye Saves the World, Arthur, Q N’ A with Mikki and Shay, Jimmy Kimmel Live!, Paradise Run, and Home & Family. Moreover, Aubrey was also cast as Emma Stelzer in the short sci-fi drama movie, Distance, in 2014, starring alongside actors Valentino del Toro, Bel Deliá, and Geoffrey Arend. She also runs a YouTube channel with her mother which is named FoodMania Review and has accumulated more than 18k followers and more than 1.5 million views. Aubrey also enjoys a huge social media fan base and has garnered more than 250k followers on Instagram and more than 120k followers on Twitter.

First TV Show

Aubrey Anderson-Emmons made her first TV show appearance as Lily Tucker-Pritchett, Mitchell & Cameron’s adoptive daughter, in an episode, titled When Good Kids Go Bad, of the mockumentary family romantic comedy series, Modern Family, in September 2011. The show first aired on September 23, 2009, and Aubrey starred alongside the likes of Ed O’Neill, Sofía Vergara, Julie Bowen, Ty Burrell, Jesse Tyler Ferguson, Eric Stonestreet, Sarah Hyland, Ariel Winter, Nolan Gould, Rico Rodriguez, and Jeremy Maguire. As of 2019, Modern Family has been ABC’s longest-running comedy series after being on air for 10 years, starting in September 2009.

As a voice actress, Aubrey Anderson-Emmons made her TV show debut in an episode, titled Let’s Make a Choice /Something About Edward, of the Canadian/American children’s animated educational series, Arthur, in 2016.
